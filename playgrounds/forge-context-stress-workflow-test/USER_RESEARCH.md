# User Research Notes

These notes are condensed from five student interviews.

## Repeated patterns

- Students lose track of work after interruptions and busy weeks.
- They want one place to see “what matters next,” not many views.
- They dislike complex setup and stop using tools that require too much maintenance.
- Many users already keep rough task lists in notes apps and calendars.
- Several participants said they trust local tools more for personal planning than tools that feel “school-admin heavy.”

## Strongly expressed needs

- quick weekly overview
- easy recovery after missing a few days
- ability to mark uncertainty, not just completion
- soft nudges are acceptable; noisy reminders are not

## Important tensions

- Some users want automatic deadline import if it is low-friction.
- Some users explicitly do not want account creation.
- Several users like calendar export, but not mandatory calendar dependence.
- Users want enough structure to recover, but not enough structure to feel judged.

## User language worth preserving

- “Show me what I’m likely to regret ignoring.”
- “I need to recover fast after a bad week.”
- “I don’t want another app that feels like homework.”
