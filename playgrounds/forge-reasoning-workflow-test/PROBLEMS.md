# Reasoning Test Problems

Solve these exactly. Prefer clean algebra and explicit arithmetic over shortcuts that hide the logic.

## Group A: Independent Arithmetic

1. Compute:
   \[
   \frac{3}{4} + \frac{5}{6} - \frac{7}{12}
   \]

2. Compute:
   \[
   18^2 - 17^2
   \]

3. Compute:
   \[
   \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{9}{14} \div \frac{3}{7}
   \]

4. Find the greatest common divisor of `252` and `198`.

## Group B: Independent Algebra

1. Solve for `x`:
   \[
   3x - 7 = 2x + 11
   \]

2. Solve for `x`:
   \[
   x^2 - 9x + 20 = 0
   \]

3. If `2a + 3b = 19` and `a - b = 2`, find `a` and `b`.

## Group C: Review And Extension

Only tackle this after Groups A and B are complete.

1. How many integers from `1` to `120` are divisible by `4` or `6`?

2. A number is chosen from `1` to `90`. What is the probability it is divisible by `3` but not by `5`? Give the reduced fraction.

3. Write a short note identifying at least two reusable solving patterns that appeared across the earlier groups.
